The Physics of Roller Coasters

Roller coasters are some of the most popular and exciting amusement park rides. But how do they work?

Roller coasters use a combination of potential and kinetic energy to propel riders along the track.

The coaster starts by climbing a steep hill, using a motor to gain potential energy as it ascends. At the top of the hill, the coaster has stored a large amount of potential energy that is converted into kinetic energy as it begins to descend.
